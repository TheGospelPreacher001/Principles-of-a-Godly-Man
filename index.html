Principles of a Godly Man

  • 1. Put God First in Everything – “But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you.” (Matthew 6:33)
  • 2. Be Faithful in Prayer and Bible Study – “Pray without ceasing.” (1 Thessalonians 5:17); “Study to shew thyself approved unto God.” (2 Timothy 2:15)
  • 3. Preach the Truth Without Compromise – “Cry aloud, spare not, lift up thy voice like a trumpet.” (Isaiah 58:1)
  • 4. Live Above Reproach – “A bishop then must be blameless.” (1 Timothy 3:2)
  • 5. Guard Your Speech – “Let your speech be alway with grace, seasoned with salt.” (Colossians 4:6)
  • 6. Be Honest and Keep Your Word – “Providing for honest things, not only in the sight of the Lord, but also in the sight of men.” (2 Corinthians 8:21)
  • 7. Treat Women with Respect and Purity – “I made a covenant with mine eyes; why then should I think upon a maid?” (Job 31:1)
  • 8. Defend the Sanctity of Life – “Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee.” (Jeremiah 1:5)
  • 9. Honor Your Parents and Elders – “Honour thy father and thy mother.” (Exodus 20:12)
  • 10. Dress Modestly and Respectfully – “In like manner also, that women adorn themselves in modest apparel” (1 Timothy 2:9); A gentleman and pastor should be neat and presentable.
  • 11. Be Punctual and Reliable – “Let all things be done decently and in order.” (1 Corinthians 14:40)
  • 12. Be a Humble Servant – “But he that is greatest among you shall be your servant.” (Matthew 23:11)
  • 13. Show Kindness and Patience – “The servant of the Lord must not strive; but be gentle unto all men.” (2 Timothy 2:24)
  • 14. Keep Yourself from Sinful Entertainment – “I will set no wicked thing before mine eyes.” (Psalm 101:3)
  • 15. Avoid the Appearance of Evil – “Abstain from all appearance of evil.” (1 Thessalonians 5:22)
  • 16. Defend the Faith Boldly – “Earnestly contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.” (Jude 1:3)
  • 17. Be a Man of Your Word – “Swear not, neither by heaven, neither by the earth, neither by any other oath: but let your yea be yea; and your nay, nay.” (James 5:12)
  • 18. Lead by Example – “Neither as being lords over God’s heritage, but being ensamples to the flock.” (1 Peter 5:3)
  • 19. Practice Self-Control – “He that hath no rule over his own spirit is like a city that is broken down, and without walls.” (Proverbs 25:28)
  • 20. Honor the Lord in Your Finances – “Honour the Lord with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase.” (Proverbs 3:9)
  • 21. Stay Separate from Worldliness – “Come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord.” (2 Corinthians 6:17)
  • 22. Never Engage in Gossip or Slander – “A talebearer revealeth secrets: but he that is of a faithful spirit concealeth the matter.” (Proverbs 11:13)
  • 23. Walk with Integrity in All Areas of Life – “The just man walketh in his integrity: his children are blessed after him.” (Proverbs 20:7)
  • 24. Be Diligent in Your Calling – “Not slothful in business; fervent in spirit; serving the Lord.” (Romans 12:11)
  • 25. Never Let Your Emotions Control You – “He that is slow to anger is better than the mighty.” (Proverbs 16:32)
  • 26. Defend the Helpless and Stand for Justice – “Open thy mouth, judge righteously, and plead the cause of the poor and needy.” (Proverbs 31:9)
  • 27. Be a Gentleman in All Situations – Open doors for others, be polite, and respect authority.
  • 28. Control Your Eating and Drinking Habits – “And put a knife to thy throat, if thou be a man given to appetite.” (Proverbs 23:2)
  • 29. Never Be Lazy – “Go to the ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise.” (Proverbs 6:6)
  • 30. Always Be Ready to Give an Answer – “Be ready always to give an answer to every man that asketh you a reason of the hope that is in you.” (1 Peter 3:15)
  • 31. Guard Your Heart – “Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it are the issues of life.” (Proverbs 4:23)
